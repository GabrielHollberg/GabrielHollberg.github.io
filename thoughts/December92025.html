Well, it's been what? 4 days since I last wrote? And guess what? I actually finished that Software Engineering class I mentioned last time!

That's fairly impressive, it sometimes takes me a long time before I get to things. Anyway, I guess writing it down was actually super helpful. I believe that we have the potential to accomplish so much. The key is wanting to do it.

I mean think about it. How much are you willing to do to get something you really want? And I don't mean that in a weak form of want, but a strong true form of want. What you truly want you get. We just sometimes believe we want things that we don't actually want. If you truly want something you will do anything for it. Anything. If you truly want money you will work hard enough to earn it and if that doesn't work you will steal from others. If you aren't willing to do either of these things you must want something else and not truly want money. What you really want might be to relax rather than to work hard or to not hurt others instead of stealing from them.

You always get what you truly want, you just sometimes want weakly against your own true strong wants.

"Ask and ye shall recieve"
